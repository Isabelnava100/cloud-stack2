Frequently Asked Questions

Dental implants consist of titanium posts inserted into the jawbone, acting as artificial roots for teeth. They support crowns or bridges to offer a lasting remedy for missing teeth.

With adequate maintenance, dental implants can have a lifespan of many years, even a lifetime, with regular dental visits and proper oral hygiene as crucial factors.

The All-on-4 process refers to inserting four implants to hold a full set of new teeth in place, delivering a stable and practical solution for significant dental loss.

Many patients experience slight discomfort during and post-treatment since local anesthesia and sedation are used to maintain comfort throughout the procedure.

Candidates for dental implants typically have good oral health, adequate jawbone substance, and are committed to maintaining good hygiene practices.

What is the correct way to care for my dental implants?
Taking care of dental implants involves regular brushing and flossing as you would with natural teeth, alongside ongoing dental cleanings, and it's advisable to refrain from tobacco use.

Can I receive dental implants if I have a history of gum disease?
It is crucial to address any gum disease before opting for dental implants, as sound gum health is necessary for the success of implant placement.
