Oppression claims typically arise when minority shareholders are treated in a way that is unfair, prejudicial or oppressive. How long does it take to resolve an oppression claim?
The timeframe depends on the complexity of the dispute, the amount of evidence involved and whether the parties are willing to negotiate. Some matters resolve within weeks through early negotiation or mediation, while others may take several months if court proceedings are required. Once we review your situation, we can give you a clearer expectation based on your specific circumstances.

Is it common to go to court for oppression claims?
Many oppression disputes are resolved through negotiation, mediation or a structured buyout without needing a full court hearing. However, if the conduct is serious, ongoing or the majority shareholders refuse to engage constructively, court intervention may be necessary. Our commercial litigation team will advise you on the most efficient pathway and only recommend litigation when it is the strongest strategic option.
