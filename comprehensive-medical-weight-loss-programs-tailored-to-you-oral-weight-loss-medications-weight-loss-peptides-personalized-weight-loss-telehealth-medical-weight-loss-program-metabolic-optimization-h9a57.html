Frequently Asked Questions

A medical weight loss program is a structured approach to weight management guided by healthcare professionals. It may include the use of medications, dietary modifications, lifestyle changes, and regular assessments to help individuals achieve their weight loss goals.

Oral weight loss medications work by various mechanisms, such as reducing appetite, increasing satiety, or enhancing metabolism. These medications are used as part of a comprehensive strategy tailored to each individual's needs.

Weight loss peptides are generally considered safe when prescribed by qualified professionals. They work by targeting specific mechanisms involved in weight regulation and are integrated into a comprehensive weight loss plan.

Metabolic optimization involves tailoring treatments and lifestyle changes to improve your metabolic health, allowing for more effective weight management. It encompasses a range of strategies, including diet, exercise, and medication.

Telehealth offers a convenient way to access medical expertise from home, making it easier to receive personalized support and adjustments to your weight loss program without traveling.
