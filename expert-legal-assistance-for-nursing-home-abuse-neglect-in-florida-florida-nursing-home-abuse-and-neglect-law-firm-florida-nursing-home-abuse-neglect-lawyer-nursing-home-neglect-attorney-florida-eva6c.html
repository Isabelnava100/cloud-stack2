Oftentimes, signs of neglect are overlooked. It is crucial to be vigilant for these red flags and to act promptly if you notice them:

  • Incidents of Falling & Bone Breakage: Inadequate lighting, disarray, unavailable safety features, improper bed/wheelchair adjustments, or a lack of support from staff.
  • Pressure Sores: Typically avoidable through appropriate shifting, nutrition, and cleanliness—steps that must be noted in the care plan. Advanced cases can lead to severe conditions such as osteomyelitis or sepsis.
  • Malnutrition and Dehydration: Conditions that indicate absent basic care and oversight—significant warning signals, particularly if indicated on a death certificate.
  • Wandering & Elopement: Inadequate supervision in memory-care or supposedly “secured” units, resulting in potential injuries or threats to safety.

Frequently Asked Questions

Abuse in nursing homes can manifest as physical violence, emotional damage, or neglect that puts residents' safety at risk. Warning signs may include physical injuries, drastic shifts in behavior, or lack of proper care.

A skilled attorney in nursing home neglect can assess your claims, collect relevant evidence, negotiate with insurers, and represent your interests in court, striving for justice and remuneration for your loved ones.

In Florida, the timeframe for pursuing a nursing home abuse claim is generally two years from the occurrence of the injury or incident, making it imperative to act without delay.

Look out for red flags such as unexplained injuries, social withdrawal, sudden mood changes, and neglect indicators like inadequate hygiene. Additionally, be attentive to withdrawal, signs of anxiety or depression, and unusual fear around staff (e.g., flinching, inability or reluctance to communicate). Changes in sleeping patterns or appetite and restrictions like limited visitor interaction or phone communication are also alarming signs.
