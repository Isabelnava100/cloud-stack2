Frequently Asked Questions

Obstetric ultrasound refers to an imaging technique employed to check on a fetus's progress and health throughout pregnancy.

Generally, your initial ultrasound takes place between the 6-8 week mark of your pregnancy, with subsequent scans recommended at different stages.

Indeed, gynaecological ultrasound concentrates on the female reproductive system while obstetric ultrasound is focused on pregnancy monitoring and fetal growth.

Most ultrasound sessions last between 30 minutes to an hour, varying by the ultrasound type performed.

Certainly, after your ultrasound, you will obtain a report that summarises the findings, which you can forward to your healthcare provider.

Is there any safety concern linked with ultrasound procedures?
Ultrasound is regarded as a secure imaging approach and involves no known dangers, utilizing sound waves rather than radiation.

How should I prepare for my ultrasound session?
Preparation differs; for obstetric ultrasounds, it might require that you hydrate beforehand to ensure a full bladder, contrasting with varied instructions for gynaecological ultrasounds.
