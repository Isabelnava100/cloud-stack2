Understanding Disease Transfer Through Pruning Tools

When pruning, it is easy to overlook the impact that contaminated shears and tools can have on your plants. These tools can transfer pathogens from one plant to another, leading to widespread disease and compromised growth. Identifying the sources of plant disease, such as fungal spores or bacterial infections, is vital for maintaining garden health. By ensuring your tools are sanitized before each use, you significantly reduce the chances of spreading diseases that can lead to poor crop yields and plant death.
