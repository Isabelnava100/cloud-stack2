Types of Extendable Dining Tables

Butterfly Extension Tables

The most popular extending dining table design features a hidden leaf stored beneath the tabletop. Simply pull the table ends apart, and the butterfly leaf unfolds automatically. Perfect for those who want a seamless, all-in-one solution.

 

Self-Storing Extension Tables

These expandable dining tables keep extension leaves neatly stored within the table frame. No need for separate storage—everything you need is built in. Ideal for apartment living where storage space is limited.

 

Drop Leaf Tables

A classic extending table design where hinged leaves fold down when not in use. These extendable kitchen tables work beautifully in compact spaces, offering maximum flexibility with a smaller footprint.

 

Separate Leaf Storage

Traditional extension dining tables with removable leaves that store separately. These offer the greatest size flexibility and are perfect for large family gatherings where you need maximum seating capacity.

Choosing the Right Size Extendable Dining Table

Measure Your Space

Allow at least 90cm (36 inches) of clearance around all sides of the table for comfortable movement and chair access.

 

Consider Your Needs

An expandable dining table that seats 4–6 people comfortably in compact mode but extends to seat 8–10 is perfect for most families.

 

Room Size Recommendations

  • Small Dining Area (Under 12m²): 120–140cm extendable table
  • Medium Dining Room (12–20m²): 140–160cm extending table
  • Large Dining Space (20m²+): 160–180cm+ expandable table
  •  

Size Guide

  • Compact Mode (Daily Use): 120cm – seats 4; 140cm – seats 4–6; 160cm – seats 6–8
  • Extended Mode (Entertaining): up to 200cm+ – seats 10–12
  • Custom sizes available on request
