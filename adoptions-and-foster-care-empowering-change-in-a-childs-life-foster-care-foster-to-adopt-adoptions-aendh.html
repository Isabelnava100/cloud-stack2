Foster Care and Adoption Services Overview

The Importance of Foster Care

Opting for foster care signifies your willingness to open your home and heart to a child in need. Numerous children in the system yearn for the loving embrace of a family. By stepping into the role of a foster parent, you play a pivotal part in aiding these children to heal and flourish. Foster care encompasses more than just providing a refuge; it aims to nurture their emotional and psychological health.

Advantages of Adoption

Adoption offers a loving, permanent solution for children unable to stay with their biological families. It fosters enduring family ties and grants children the opportunity to grow up in a supportive, stable home. Kids who are adopted often thrive emotionally and developmentally in nurturing homes. Welcoming a child through adoption can be a transformative experience for both the adoptive family and the child.

Pathway to Foster-to-Adopt

The foster-to-adopt model allows homes to initially foster a child with the intent to adopt them, provided circumstances are favorable. This unique pathway ensures stability for children while they build connections with their foster families, awaiting legal finality for adoption. It grants families the joys of fostering and allows them to positively impact the child's future success.

Frequently Asked Questions

Foster care refers to a temporary placement for a child in a secure home when their biological family is unable to support them appropriately.

The adoption procedure generally involves submitting an application, completing training and evaluations, and then the process of matching with a child who is prepared for adoption.

Eligibility criteria can differ, but typically include being a responsible, healthy adult aged 21 or older, possessing stable income, dependable transportation, and successfully passing background screenings.

Foster care is a temporary arrangement that may lead to family reunification, while adoption establishes a permanent family relationship.

We prioritize the interest of keeping biological siblings unified in foster care whenever it is feasible to help maintain their family connections.
