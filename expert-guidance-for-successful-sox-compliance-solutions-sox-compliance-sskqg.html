Understanding SOX Compliance Requirements

SOX compliance refers to the regulations established under the Sarbanes-Oxley Act aimed at protecting investors by improving the accuracy and reliability of corporate disclosures. Companies preparing for an IPO or existing public entities must adhere to strict internal controls and financial reporting standards. Our experts guide clients through understanding each section of SOX, focusing on 404A and 404B compliance specifics to align their business processes accordingly.

What is the difference between SOX 404A and SOX 404B?
SOX 404A applies to emerging growth companies, allowing for less stringent requirements, while SOX 404B applies to larger accelerated filers and mandates an independent auditor's report on internal controls.

How often should we review our SOX compliance status?
It is advisable to review your SOX compliance status at least annually, or following any significant operational changes or business expansions.
