Understanding What Spinal Decompression Is

Spinal decompression therapy is a controlled, computer-assisted technique that gently stretches the spine. This slow and precise movement alters the position and force of the spine, creating space between the vertebrae and relieving pressure on compressed discs and nerves. As the spine stretches, pressure is reduced within the spinal discs, allowing bulging or herniated discs to retract. This process enhances the movement of water, oxygen, and nutrients into the discs, promoting healing in areas that might otherwise struggle to recover on their own.

This therapy is performed on a specialized decompression table and can be customized to focus on either the lumbar or cervical spine, depending on the patient’s symptoms and diagnosis. When administered by experienced professionals, it is safe, comfortable, and suitable for people of various ages and conditions.

Common Conditions That Benefit from Decompression

Many patients in Cornelius seek decompression therapy because they are suffering from conditions that cause persistent back or neck pain. Herniated and bulging discs are among the most common causes of spinal discomfort, as they can press against nearby nerves and cause pain to radiate throughout the body. Degenerative disc disease, which occurs as the discs naturally wear down over time, can also be treated effectively through decompression. Conditions like sciatica, where pain travels down the leg due to nerve irritation, often respond well to spinal traction. Others find relief from symptoms related to spinal stenosis, facet joint dysfunction, or general nerve impingement.

By creating negative pressure within the spinal discs, decompression therapy offers a way to reduce nerve pressure, restore disc height, and support natural healing processes. This can significantly reduce pain and restore function in patients who have previously found little success with traditional therapies.

How Decompression Works During Treatment

At Lakeside Sports Chiropractic and Physical Therapy, we begin with a thorough evaluation of your spine, health history, and any imaging you’ve done in the past. Once we determine you’re a good candidate for spinal decompression, a personalized treatment plan is developed. Each session involves lying on a decompression table while a computer-controlled mechanism delivers gentle, intermittent traction to the spine. The treatment typically lasts between 20 and 30 minutes, depending on your condition and tolerance.

The process alternates between phases of stretching and relaxation, which mimic natural spinal movements and help encourage rehydration and healing. Over time, this therapy can reduce inflammation, increase flexibility, and promote long-term relief from spinal compression. Many patients report a sense of relief after just a few sessions, and results typically improve with consistent care.
