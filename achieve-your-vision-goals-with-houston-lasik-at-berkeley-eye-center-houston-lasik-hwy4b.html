Frequently Asked Questions

LASIK eye surgery is a popular refractive surgery that corrects vision problems such as nearsightedness, farsightedness, and astigmatism. The procedure involves reshaping the cornea using a laser to improve the eye's ability to focus light onto the retina. At Berkeley Eye Center, our experienced surgeons utilize advanced technology to ensure precise and effective results, making us a leading choice for Houston LASIK.

During LASIK eye surgery, a thin flap is created on the surface of the cornea using a microkeratome or a femtosecond laser. This flap is then lifted to allow the laser to reshape the underlying corneal tissue. After the laser treatment, the flap is repositioned, and the eye begins to heal quickly. Patients often experience improved vision within a day, making LASIK eye surgery in Houston a convenient option for those seeking vision correction.

Good candidates for LASIK eye surgery typically include individuals over the age of 18 with a stable prescription for at least one year. Candidates should also have healthy corneas and no significant eye conditions.
