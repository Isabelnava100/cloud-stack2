Frequently Asked Questions

Dental implants are synthetic tooth roots that provide permanent support for fixed or removable replacement teeth.

The entire dental implant process may span several months, including healing time; however, the surgical part typically takes just a couple of hours.

Most patients experience minimal discomfort during the procedure, often effectively managed with anesthesia and follow-up pain-relieving medications.

People with missing teeth and adequate bone density for implant support are usually ideal candidates for this procedure.

Caring for dental implants involves good oral hygiene practices, regular dental check-ups, and refraining from hard foods that may harm the implants.
