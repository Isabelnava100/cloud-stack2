Understanding Dental Implants

Dental implants consist of titanium rods that act as artificial tooth roots for missing teeth, providing a strong and permanent base for replacement teeth or dental bridges. Unlike removable dentures, these implants are securely integrated into the jawbone, offering unmatched stability. At Cypress Dental, we employ cutting-edge technology to determine your suitability for dental implants and develop tailored treatment plans for optimum outcomes. Frequently Asked Questions

Dental implants are titanium fixtures that are surgically implanted into the jawbone, acting as artificial tooth roots. Once they heal, they create a reliable base for artificial teeth.

Ideal candidates for dental implants are those with healthy gums, sufficient bone density, and no medical conditions that could interfere with the healing process.

Typically, the dental implant process consists of several steps, including evaluation, placement, and recovery, which can range from a few months to a year, depending on the individual case.

Some discomfort may occur during and following the procedure; however, local anesthesia is administered to reduce pain, and additional pain management options are also available as required.

The success rate of dental implants is approximately 95% when properly placed and maintained, establishing them as a dependable option for those with missing teeth.
