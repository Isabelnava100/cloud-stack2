Common Questions

ISO certification is the official affirmation that our organization’s management system adheres to global standards like ISO 9001 (Quality), ISO 14001 (Environmental), or ISO 45001 (Occupational Health & Safety). Its importance lies in showcasing reliable, regulated operations, fostering client and regulator trust, and often is a requirement for access to tenders, contracts, and supply networks.

IntegriSURE provides a well-organized, self-guided approach that empowers our organization to develop, implement, and maintain an ISO-compatible framework without the need for conventional consultant-centric models. Instead of beginning from the ground up to interpret standards, we traverse a distinct framework equipped with templates, methodical implementation, and tools that enhance practical adoption and preparation for audits.

IntegriSURE assists in creating integrated management systems tied to: - ISO 9001 (Quality Management) - ISO 14001 (Environmental Management) - ISO 45001 (Occupational Health & Safety) These standards can be implemented separately or as part of a sole Integrated Management System (IMS).

ISO certification holds particular significance for SMEs aiming to: - Qualify for tenders with government and enterprise - Enhance operational reliability and accountability - Demonstrate adherence to regulatory or contractual obligations - Build credibility with customers and stakeholders Such certifications are relevant across various sectors, including construction, service industries, manufacturing, and healthcare support.

The timeframe for achieving ISO certification differs based on the state of our organization’s current systems and the certification scope. Most SMEs may anticipate a duration of 3 to 12 months, differing according to existing documentation, processes, availability of resources, and methodical implementation.
