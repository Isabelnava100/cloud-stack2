Additional FAQ

What do customs-supervised warehouse standards entail?

Customs-supervised warehouse requirements necessitate a rigorous system for inventory recordkeeping, adherence to customs standards, and cooperation with inspections mandated by customs authorities.

How can I identify trustworthy 3PL with FTZ functionalities?

With WareMatch, finding dependable 3PL options with FTZ functionalities becomes simpler, offering seamless support to locate appropriate storage partners suited to operational requirements.

Can you explain what an FTZ is and how it assists importers?

A Foreign Trade Zone (FTZ) is a sanctioned area by Customs and Border Protection (CBP) that allows for the storage, processing, or manufacturing of both foreign and domestically sourced goods without undergoing the typical customs entry protocol. Utilizing an FTZ benefits importers through duty deferral, inverted tariff structures (favorable for finished goods), and substantial MPF savings through weekly consolidated entries.

What constitutes a CBP Bonded Warehouse, and how does it contrast with an FTZ?

A CBP Bonded Warehouse is a regulated establishment where goods can be stored for a duration not exceeding 5 years without the obligation to pay duties. Key distinctions include that bonded facilities are restricted to storage and minimal modifications (like sorting or repackaging), while FTZs offer comprehensive operations including full production and assembly. With FTZs, there are also MPF savings available through consolidated entry submissions not found in bonded setups. Further details can be found on WareMatch.

In what way does duty deferral function within an FTZ or bonded store?

Duty deferral permits companies to postpone customs duty payments until their goods depart from the zone, entering into U.S. commerce. Should items be directly exported from an FTZ or bonded warehouse to overseas, U.S. duty payments are avoided. This significantly boosts cash flow by aligning duty obligations with actual sale occurrences.

Can you explain duty inversion and its applicability within an FTZ?

Duty inversion, exclusive to FTZs, permits businesses a lower duty rate for finished goods over potentially higher rates for their component parts. For instance, if component parts come with a 6% duty while the final finished product holds a 0% rate, the manufacturer would only incur a 0% basis when the product enters U.S. commerce, thus completely negating component tariff obligations.

How can an FTZ impact and lessen Merchandise Processing Fees (MPF)?

In an FTZ, importers can benefit greatly from the "weekly entry" system, allowing a consolidated CBP entry brief for withdrawals on a weekly basis. This contrasts sharply with the necessity to file entry documents per container shipment—cappping the MPF minimum obligation to around $614.35 weekly. For importers with high volumes, this can result in annual savings amounting to millions compared to individually handled MPF.

What compliance measures are needed from FTZ operators under CBP regulations?

Operators within FTZs must adapt to CBP-approved inventory control systems and rigorous recordkeeping measures (such as ACE), conduct comprehensive annual reviews, and enable CBP accessibility for inspections round the clock. Facilities need to adhere to precise physical security guidelines such as erecting secure fences, organizing access points, and implementing personnel verification processes.

Can you describe the activation procedure for FTZ?

Activation embodies the conclusive stage where CBP endorses a site for FTZ functionalities. It necessitates an official application along with rigorous site inspections and confirmation of the inventory control architecture. Businesses collaborating through WareMatch that utilize established FTZ-active 3PL partners have the assurance of instantaneous commencement of operations since they have met all requisite criteria.

What types of operations can be done with goods in an FTZ?

FTZs offer unmatched operational flexibility, encompassing storage, displays, modifications (including relabeling/packaging), and manufacturing processes. Additionally, goods can shift from one FTZ to another (known as zone-to-zone movement) without triggering tariffs or requiring standard customs processing.

In what ways does FTZ warehousing enhance e-commerce import dynamics?

E-commerce brands find FTZs advantageous for maintaining bulk inventory under duty-deferring conditions, incurring duties solely on individualized orders directed to local customers. This model proves especially beneficial for companies dealing with high-value imports or those that route segments of their stock to foreign entities, avoiding U.S. duties for goods that are re-exported.
