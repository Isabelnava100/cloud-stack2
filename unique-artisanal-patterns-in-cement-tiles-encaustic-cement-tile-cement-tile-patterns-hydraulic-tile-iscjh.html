Frequently Asked Questions

Cement tile patterns, commonly known as hydraulic tiles, are artisanal creations featuring intricate patterns and flexible color options, suitable for distinct spaces.

Hydraulic tiles are produced by combining cement, pigments, and hydraulic pressure to form sturdy, decorative tiles that bypass the need for firing during production.

Encaustic cement tile comprises a type of cement tile decorated with patterns formed by embedding colored clay into the tile's surface, creating vivid and intricate designs.

Cement tiles are versatile enough to be utilized in kitchens, bathrooms, flooring, walls, and outdoor settings, offering an attractive and robust surface for numerous applications.

Installing cement tiles requires preparing the base, applying thin-set mortar, and meticulously placing the tiles. Please refer to our in-depth installation guide to ensure successful adherence.

Are cement tiles durable?
Absolutely, cement tiles are revered for their robust durability and longevity, making them an ideal option for high-traffic environments.

What maintenance do cement tiles require?
To maintain the appearance and extend the lifespan of your cement tiles, regular cleaning with mild soap and water, alongside periodic sealing, is recommended.
