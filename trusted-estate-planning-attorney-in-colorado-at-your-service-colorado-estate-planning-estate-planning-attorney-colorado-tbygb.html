Common Questions

Estate planning is the process of arranging for the management and distribution of your assets upon your death or incapacity. It includes creating wills, trusts, and other legal documents.

An estate planning attorney in Colorado can provide invaluable expertise on state laws and help you create a personalized plan that ensures your assets are protected and distributed according to your wishes.

We offer a wide range of services including wills, trusts, powers of attorney, living wills, and strategies for asset protection tailored to meet your individual needs.

In Colorado, probate is a legal process that oversees the distribution of a deceased person's estate. It involves validating the will, identifying assets, paying debts, and distributing remaining assets to beneficiaries.

A will directs how your assets will be distributed after your death, while a trust allows you to manage and protect your assets during your lifetime and beyond. Trusts can also help avoid probate.
