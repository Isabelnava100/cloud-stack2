Rug tufting involves using a tufting gun to insert yarn into a base fabric, creating a pile that forms the rug's design. The process starts by securing the backing material onto a rug tufting frame. The tufting gun shoots yarn through the backing to create loops that can be cut or left as is, depending on the desired texture. Once the tufting is complete, a final backing is added to provide durability and finish the rug. This technique allows for endless customization in color, design, and size.

A rug tufting frame is a sturdy structure designed to hold your fabric taut while you tuft your rug. It keeps the backing material stable, making it easier to work with and ensuring precise tufting.
