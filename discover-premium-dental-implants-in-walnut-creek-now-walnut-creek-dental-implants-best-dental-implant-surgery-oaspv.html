Frequently Asked Questions

Dental implants are titanium posts inserted into the jawbone which act as a stable base for replacement teeth.

Candidates for dental implants typically include those who have lost teeth, possess adequate bone density, and enjoy overall good health.

While the duration can differ, the dental implant procedure generally takes around 1-2 hours for placement, with further time needed for full recovery.

Initial recovery lasts a few days, yet complete healing may span several months.

The majority of patients report experiencing minimal discomfort during and after the procedure, which can be addressed with recommended pain relief.

How should I care for my dental implants?
Maintaining dental implants involves practices similar to those used for natural teeth, including regular brushing, flossing, and attending dental check-ups.

What options are available if I lack sufficient bone for implants?
In cases of insufficient bone, alternatives such as bone grafting can be explored to support dental implants.
