Invisalign for Adolescents: The Perfect Solution

Why Opt for Invisalign for Teens?

At Sakowitz Smiles in Winter Garden, FL, Invisalign is preferred for adolescents as it provides numerous advantages over traditional braces. Firstly, the aligners are nearly invisible, helping teens showcase their confidence during treatment. Moreover, being removable makes it more convenient for them to care for their teeth and enjoy all their beloved foods without any limitations. Lastly, Invisalign prioritize comfort, enabling teens to engage in daily pursuits, such as sports and events, without the discomfort or irritation common with braces.

Advantages of Invisalign Treatment

Invisalign treatment in Winter Garden FL not only improves appearance but supports better oral health. Because there are no brackets or wires like those used in classic braces, the chances of cavities or gum disease decrease significantly, as teens can easily brush and floss their teeth. Additionally, advanced 3D technology allows for personalized treatment planning, so adjustments can be made with precision. At Sakowitz Smiles, we focus on each patient's individual requirements to create a customized plan for exceptional outcomes.

What to Anticipate During Invisalign Treatment

Upon beginning Invisalign treatment in Winter Garden FL, patients undergo a thorough analysis followed by a bespoke treatment strategy. Generally, each set of aligners is to be worn 20 to 22 hours per day, and aligners are usually swapped out bi-weekly for progressive tooth movement. Dr. Sakowitz and the team will consistently monitor the progress during routine visits. With open communication and ongoing support, we accompany teens on their journey toward achieving an enviable smile.

Extra FAQ

How often do I need to change my aligners?

Aligners are generally swapped out every week to two weeks, depending upon Dr. Sakowitz's prescribed treatment plan.

Can I play sports while wearing Invisalign?

Absolutely! Since Invisalign aligners are designed to be taken out, it is safe to remove them during sports, reducing the risk of injury.
