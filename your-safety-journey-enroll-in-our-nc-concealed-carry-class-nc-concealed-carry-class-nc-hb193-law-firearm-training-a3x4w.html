Insights Into NC HB193 Law and Its Relevance

The NC HB193 law outlines the prerequisites and laws pertaining to obtaining a permit to carry concealed weapons in North Carolina. Are additional training hours needed to comply with NC HB193?
Yes, an additional 8-9 hours of NRA-approved training is necessary to align with North Carolina's requirements. A. C. Johnson, an instructor with Nu Day Arms Training, is certified to teach both NC concealed carry handgun training and NRA safety courses, ensuring compliance with NC HB193.

Do I need to bring my own firearm to the class?
Participants are encouraged to bring their own firearms for the hands-on training sessions, although options for rentals may be made available.

What happens after I complete the class?
Upon finishing the class, students are awarded a certification that allows them to submit an application for a concealed carry permit in North Carolina.
