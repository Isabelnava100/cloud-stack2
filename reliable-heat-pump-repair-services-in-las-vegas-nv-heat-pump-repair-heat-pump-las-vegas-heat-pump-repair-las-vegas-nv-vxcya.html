Common Heat Pump Issues We Handle

Heat pumps are complex systems with various components, and when something goes wrong, it can affect your home’s comfort. Here are some common heat pump problems that our technicians encounter and repair:

  • Insufficient Heating or Cooling: If your heat pump isn’t providing the right amount of heating or cooling, it could be due to a malfunctioning thermostat, refrigerant leaks, or a clogged air filter.
  • Unusual Noises: Strange sounds such as grinding, rattling, or hissing can indicate a problem with the motor, fan blades, or refrigerant levels.
  • Frozen Coils: In winter, your heat pump’s coils can freeze up, preventing proper heating. This could be caused by restricted airflow, low refrigerant, or a malfunctioning defrost cycle.
  • Short Cycling: If your heat pump constantly turns on and off without completing a full heating or cooling cycle, it could be due to an electrical issue or an oversized system.
  • Increased Energy Bills: A sudden spike in your energy bills could indicate that your heat pump is working harder than it should due to inefficiencies or malfunctioning components.

Our team at 32 Degrees is trained to quickly diagnose and fix these problems, restoring your system to full functionality.
