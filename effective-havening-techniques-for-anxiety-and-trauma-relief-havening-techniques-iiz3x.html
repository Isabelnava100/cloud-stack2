Understanding Havening Techniques for Healing

What are Havening Techniques?

Havening Techniques are a set of therapeutic methods I use every day in my clinic to help people release the emotional charge from painful or traumatic memories. Put simply, they allow the brain to let go of the “alarm signal” that keeps past experiences feeling present. The process combines soothing touch on the arms, hands, and face with gentle distraction exercises like counting, visualisation, or humming. This creates calming brainwave activity (known as delta waves) that signals to the amygdala — the part of the brain that stores emotional memories — that the threat is over. Once that happens, the emotional intensity linked to the memory can dissolve. What I love about Havening is that it’s not about reliving trauma or digging endlessly into the past. Instead, it gives your brain a chance to re-code the memory at a neurochemical level, so the old fear, panic, or shame no longer gets triggered. Clients often describe it as if a weight has been lifted, or as finally feeling safe inside their own body again.

How Havening Works

When we go through something overwhelming, the brain encodes that experience with strong emotions like fear, panic, or shame. These get “hard-wired” into the amygdala — the part of the brain that’s always scanning for danger. That’s why people often find themselves reacting as if the past is still happening, even when they logically know they’re safe. Havening interrupts that process in a very gentle way. By using calming touch along with simple mental tasks, we generate delta waves — the same slow brainwaves linked to deep rest and recovery. These waves act as a kind of safety signal to the brain. When the amygdala receives this signal, the chemical bond holding that traumatic memory in place begins to unlock. In real terms, this means the memory loses its painful emotional charge. You might still remember what happened, but the fear, panic, or distress no longer hijacks your system. Over the years I’ve guided thousands of clients through this process, and the change is often immediate. People who came in shaking with anxiety have walked out feeling calm and centred — sometimes within a single session.

So whether you’re carrying deep trauma or just dealing with everyday pressures, Havening Techniques can be adapted to meet you where you are. It’s both a rapid relief method and a path to lasting change.
