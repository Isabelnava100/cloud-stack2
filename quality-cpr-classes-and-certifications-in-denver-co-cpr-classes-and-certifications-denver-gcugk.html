Frequently Asked Questions

CPR classes are structured training sessions aimed at teaching individuals how to perform cardiopulmonary resuscitation (CPR) effectively. These classes cover critical skills such as chest compressions, rescue breaths, and the use of an Automated External Defibrillator (AED). Certifications, such as the BLS CPR certification, confirm that a participant has successfully completed the training and is capable of performing CPR in emergency situations. In Denver, CO, we provide American Heart Association (AHA) certified CPR classes and certifications to ensure you receive the highest quality training.

The CPR certification process usually involves attending a CPR class that includes both theoretical knowledge and practical skills. During the class, participants learn about the significance of CPR, how to assess an emergency situation, and the steps to perform CPR effectively. After completing the training, participants must pass a skills test and a written exam to receive their BLS CPR certification. What is the difference between BLS and CPR certification?
BLS (Basic Life Support) certification is a specialized form of CPR certification that emphasizes providing care to patients in emergency scenarios, particularly within healthcare environments. BLS training covers CPR techniques for adults, children, and infants, along with the use of an AED. In contrast, standard CPR certification may not encompass all elements of BLS.
