Comprehending Sleep Apnea and Your Treatment Choices

Defining Obstructive Sleep Apnea

Obstructive sleep apnea (OSA) is a serious sleep condition characterized by periodic blockages of the airway during slumber. Consequently, this can result in interruptions to your breathing, which disrupts your natural sleep cycles. Common signs of OSA include heavy snoring, abrupt awakenings with shortness of breath, and excessive lethargy during the day. Identifying the symptoms of this condition is crucial for pursuing effective remedies.
