How to Cook Wagyu Beef Perfectly

Cooking wagyu beef requires a gentle touch to preserve its natural flavors. Start with a high-quality cut and allow the meat to reach room temperature before cooking. For the best results, sear wagyu beef on high heat and then finish cooking on low to medium heat. How should I store wagyu beef after purchase?
Keep wagyu beef refrigerated and consume it within a few days. For longer storage, consider freezing it to maintain freshness.

Does wagyu beef come from Japan exclusively?
While authentic Japanese wagyu is highly sought after, wagyu cattle are also raised in other countries, including the United States, where they follow similar breeding practices.
