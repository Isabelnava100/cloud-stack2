How Pressurized Climate Control Technology Makes Hyperbaric Therapy Safer for Home Users

Heat Problem in HBOT Chambers

Thermal buildup is a significant challenge faced in hyperbaric oxygen therapy (HBOT) chambers. When users are subjected to prolonged exposure to heat in these enclosed environments, it can lead to discomfort and compliance issues. Aftermarket cooling systems often fail to maintain consistent temperature control, resulting in unsafe conditions that jeopardize pressure integrity. Therefore, addressing thermal buildup efficiently is critical for ensuring that pressurized climate control meets the safety standards required for at-home hyperbaric therapy.
