Opening a company in Italy involves several steps. Initially, you need to choose the type of company structure you want, such as a società a responsabilità limitata (SRL) or a società per azioni (SPA). After determining the structure, you must draft the company's articles of association and have them notarized by a public notary. Subsequently, obtaining a tax identification number (Codice Fiscale) and registering with the Italian Companies Register (Registro delle Imprese) is necessary. You may also need to comply with specific industry regulations depending on your business activities. What costs are involved in establishing a company in Italy?
Establishing a company in Italy incurs several costs that entrepreneurs should consider. These expenses include notarization fees for the articles of association, registration fees for the Italian Companies Register, and potentially legal service fees for consultation and assistance. Additionally, you may need to deposit a minimum share capital (for example, €10,000 for an SRL) into a corporate bank account. Ongoing costs such as accounting, administrative fees, and tax obligations also need to be factored in. It is wise to create a detailed budget and consult with professionals at IACOVAZZI GLOBAL BUSINESS LAWYERS to understand the total investment required.

Can a foreigner open a company in Italy?
Yes, foreigners can open a company in Italy. Italian laws allow non-residents to establish a business, and they do not need to have a physical address in Italy before registering their company. However, it is mandatory to appoint a legal representative who is a resident in Italy for administrative purposes. Furthermore, obtaining a Codice Fiscale is crucial for tax identification. The process maintains similar requirements as for Italian citizens, including documentation and registration in the Companies Register.
