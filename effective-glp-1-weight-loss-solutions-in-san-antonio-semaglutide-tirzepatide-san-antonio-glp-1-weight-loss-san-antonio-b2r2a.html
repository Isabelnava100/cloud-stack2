Comparing Semaglutide and Tirzepatide for Weight Loss

Both semaglutide and tirzepatide function as glucagon-like peptide-1 (GLP-1) receptor agonists; however, they exhibit different properties in terms of efficacy and application. Semaglutide, approved initially for type 2 diabetes management, has demonstrated significant weight loss effects, with clinical studies showing an average loss of approximately 15% of body weight. On the other hand, tirzepatide is a dual-action medication that targets GLP-1 and GIP (glucose-dependent insulinotropic polypeptide) receptors, potentially yielding even greater weight loss results due to its enhanced mechanism. This makes tirzepatide particularly appealing for patients with obesity, as it may lead to substantial reductions in body mass index for those who qualify.
