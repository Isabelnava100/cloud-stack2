Understanding Deep Plaque and Its Implications

Plaque is a thin layer of bacteria that continuously accumulates on your teeth. If not properly managed, it hardens into tartar, which is not removable through typical brushing. A buildup of deep plaque situated beneath the gumline can result in: gum inflammation (known as gingivitis), periodontal disease, unpleasant breath, loss of bone around teeth, and tooth loss if untreated. This highlights the necessity of consistent teeth cleaning visits and why some individuals may need a more intensive periodontal cleaning.

The Process of Removing Deep Plaque by Dentists

At Progressive Dental Care, we offer a customized and contemporary approach to teeth cleaning and periodontal cleaning. Since every patient's dental situation is distinct, we assess your needs thoroughly and implement state-of-the-art technology to ensure comfort and effective care. Here's an overview of what the typical deep plaque removal procedure entails: The process starts with a comprehensive assessment of your teeth and gums performed by our professionals. Our dental hygienists will check your gum status, gauge pocket depths, and ascertain whether a standard teeth cleaning or a comprehensive periodontal cleaning is appropriate for you. Given the continuous advancements in dentistry, we tailor each cleaning procedure based on your specific oral health needs, comfort preferences, and the amount of plaque accumulation present. The subsequent phase consists of scaling, which focuses on eliminating plaque, tartar, biofilm, and surface discoloration above and below the gum line. Throughout this phase, we might utilize a cutting-edge ultrasonic cleaning device. This method leverages gentle sound wave vibrations to effectively disrupt plaque accumulation, usually allowing for fewer scraping interventions than traditional techniques. The ultrasonic tool consists of: a smooth metal tip that vibrates rapidly, a gentle water spray to clean away debris and bacteria, and dental suction to enhance comfort and hygiene. This innovative method is particularly proficient in combatting biofilm, which is the bacteria-laden plaque that can incite gum disease. For those facing gum disease, scaling also directly tackles bacteria hidden within periodontal pockets, preventing the worsening of the infection. Post-treatment, ongoing upkeep is crucial. Regular teeth cleaning sessions, diligent brushing and flossing routines, along with periodic examinations, are all essential to maintain your results and minimize the chance of future dental concerns or gum disease.
