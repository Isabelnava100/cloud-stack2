Understanding Neuropathy and Its Causes

Neuropathy refers to nerve damage that often results in pain, weakness, and numbness. Various factors contribute to this condition, including diabetes, injury, infections, and hereditary conditions. Understanding the underlying causes is crucial for effective neuropathy treatments. Frequently Asked Questions

Common symptoms of neuropathy include numbness, tingling, pain, sensitivity to touch, and weakness, often affecting the hands and feet.

Neuropathy can be caused by diabetes, infections, autoimmune diseases, exposure to toxins, trauma, and hereditary conditions.

Neuropathy is diagnosed through a physical examination, patient history, and diagnostic tests such as nerve conduction studies or blood tests.

At Neuropathy Orange County, we offer various treatments including physical therapy, medication management, and cutting-edge therapies like laser treatment.

While some forms of neuropathy can be managed or improved with treatment, complete reversal may not be possible depending on the cause.
