Care Guidelines for Your Aluminum Boat Lift

To extend the lifespan of your aluminum boat lift, consider these key maintenance steps:

Routine Inspections: Monitor cables, pulleys, and bunk alignment.
Post-Use Rinsing: Clean any salt and dragged debris using fresh water to avoid accumulation.
Grease the Movable Parts: Follow manufacturer recommendations for levers and gear lubrication.
control Testing: Confirm the functionality of the remote(s), limit switches, and safety systems.
Annual Maintenance: Schedule comprehensive professional evaluations prior to the storm season.

Frequently Asked Questions

An aluminum boat lift refers to a sophisticated docking apparatus designed to elevate and lower boats meticulously out of the water. By keeping your boat clear of the water's surface, it plays a crucial role in preventing damage from humid corrosion, algae accumulations, and incessant water movements. Aluminum lifts have become increasingly favored in Florida for their lightweight nature, resilience, and suitability for both salt and brackish water factors.

Aluminum boat lifts are highly regarded because they present a blend of strength and minimal upkeep:
• Corrosion Protection in coastal and moist climates.
• Lightweight Resilience which enhances maneuverability and carries high weight capacities.
• Low Maintenance Requirements compared to wood or steel alternatives.
• Reducing Hull Fouling by storing the boat above the water, resulting in fewer cycles needed for bottom paint upkeep.

The required size of your lift is calibrated based on your boat's dry weight (inclusive of fuel/gear), overall length, and beam width, in addition to local factors like water depth, pilings, and boathouse configurations. The safest mode of action is to conduct a professional evaluation. MacDuff Marine will inspect your conditions and suggest an appropriately rated aluminum boat lift tailored specifically for your dock and craft.

To enhance lift longevity and its performance efficiency, practice these habits:
• Post-usage Rinse with fresh water after intensive usage to eliminate leftover salt and dirt.
• Regular Checks every three months for cable deterioration, pulley efficiency, and bunk position.
• Grease Moveable Sections (pulleys/gears) as per manufacturers' recommendations.
• System Function Tests including remotes, limit switches, and security measures.
• Yearly Service—book a professional inspection ahead of the hurricane season.
Keep track of these actions to timely identify any issues and resolve them promptly.
