Dental implants serve as a durable solution for lacking teeth, often lasting many years or even a lifetime for various users. The implant, which features a robust titanium post embedded into the jawbone, is incredibly resilient and fuses with the bone over time, providing a stable support system. The lifespan of a dental implant partially relies on factors like your overall physical health, oral hygiene practices, and consistency with dental check-ups. With diligent home care, regular professional cleaning, and follow-ups, implants have a remarkably high success rate. While the attached crown may need replacement over time due to typical wear, the underlying implant post is designed for durability.
