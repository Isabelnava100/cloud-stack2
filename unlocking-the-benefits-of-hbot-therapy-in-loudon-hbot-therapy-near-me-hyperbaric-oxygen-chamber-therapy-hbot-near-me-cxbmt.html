Exploring Hyperbaric Oxygen Therapy (HBOT)

Hyperbaric Oxygen Therapy (HBOT) is a treatment modality using 100% oxygen in a controlled pressurized setting. This approach greatly enhances the quantity of oxygen that dissolves in your blood plasma. As a result, tissues benefit from improved oxygen supply, aiding in recovery and minimizing inflammation. This non-surgical method is vital for those facing chronic health challenges or individuals striving to elevate athletic capabilities. At Circulation Station, our HBOT treatments are managed by experienced professionals, ensuring utmost safety and effectiveness.

Frequently Asked Questions

Hyperbaric Oxygen Therapy (HBOT) involves employing pressurized oxygen to enhance the body's inherent healing processes by increasing oxygen delivery to affected tissues.

HBOT may assist in the recovery from various injuries, surgical procedures, chronic infections, and health conditions like traumatic brain injuries, boost recovery post-stroke, and alleviate athletic fatigue.

HBOT's mechanism involves elevating the oxygen-carrying capacity of your bloodstream, fostering cellular repair, decreasing inflammation, and enhancing overall healing.

While HBOT is generally considered safe, some individuals may feel ear pressure alterations or experience mild fatigue after treatment. Severe side effects are uncommon and often manageable.

On average, an HBOT treatment session lasts between 60 to 90 minutes, tailored according to specific protocols and individual health requirements.
