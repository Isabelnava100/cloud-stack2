How can students manage the exam stress.

Exam stress is a normal feeling for students facing board exams, and it's caused by fear of results, pressure, and overthinking, not necessarily a lack of preparation. The key to managing exam stress for student is using smart study and mental tricks: Study Smart: Use techniques like the Pomodoro Technique (short focused study with breaks) and the Blurting Method (writing down everything you know about a topic) to improve focus and spot weak areas. Also, practice sample papers at the real exam time to train your mind. Take Care of Yourself: Prioritize good sleep, eat healthy food and drink plenty of water (avoiding too much junk or caffeine), and use deep breathing exercises to calm your mind. On Exam Day: Arrive early, read the question paper for 15 minutes, and use quick stress-busters like the 5-4-3-2-1 Grounding Method or a power pose if you feel a panic attack. The Big Picture: Remember that the exam does not determine your entire future. Trust your preparation, stay focused, and believe in yourself.
