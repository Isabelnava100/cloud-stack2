Handling AI as a Change, Not a Technology Rollout

According to S&P Global Market Intelligence's 2025 survey of more than 1,000 enterprises in North America and Europe, 42% of companies abandoned most of their AI initiatives in 2025, up from 17% the year before. The technology gets installed. The behavior change does not.

At IMA Worldwide, we apply AIM (Accelerating Implementation Methodology) to AI adoption. AIM was created by Don Harrison and developed through 37+ years of implementation research at Implementation Management Associates (IMA). When AI is treated as a technology rollout rather than a behavioral change, we skip the work that makes adoption hold: sponsorship action, target readiness, and reinforcement. AIM for AI change gives a research-backed way to do that work deliberately, so the investment in the technology produces the behavior change that justifies it.

Understanding AI Change Management Principles

AI change management is the AI adoption strategy that prepares leaders, employees, and operating models to adopt AI systems successfully. Central to this approach is the AIM methodology, which emphasizes the human side of change. By identifying resistance and facilitating communication, IMA Worldwide helps businesses implement sustainable change. Understanding these principles allows leaders to steer their organizations towards success through effective AI integration.

The Importance of Human-Centric AI Approaches

AI change management places significant emphasis on human behaviors and readiness for change. By utilizing diagnostic tools such as the IRA (Individual Readiness Assessment), organizations can gauge employee preparedness and commitment. This human-centric approach ensures that workforce stakeholders feel supported and equipped to embrace new technologies and systems. IMA Worldwide's expertise in change management emphasizes building trust and instilling confidence across all levels of the organization.

Implementing AIM to Enhance Organizational Readiness

The AIM methodology offers a structured framework for managing AI implementations. It encompasses the essential non-delegable sponsor tasks critical for success. IMA Worldwide utilizes the EMR framework (Express, Model, Reinforce) to involve leaders as they effectively communicate and reinforce the change vision. By following this methodology, organizations can navigate the complexities of change with confidence, ultimately improving their overall agility and performance.
