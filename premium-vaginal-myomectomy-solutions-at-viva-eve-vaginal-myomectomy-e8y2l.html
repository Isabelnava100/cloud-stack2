Vaginal Myomectomy: Essential Insights

What is Vaginal Myomectomy?

Vaginal myomectomy is a procedure specifically designed to eliminate fibroids while preserving the uterus. This technique is favored for being minimally invasive, which affords numerous advantages such as quicker healing and reduced post-operative discomfort. The surgeon performs the procedure through the vagina, facilitating efficient removal without significant abdominal incisions. Consequently, many women experience a faster return to their routine and a remarkable enhancement in their quality of life after the surgery.

Advantages of Choosing Vaginal Myomectomy

One significant benefit of vaginal myomectomy is the ability to keep the uterus intact, thus enabling women to retain the potential for future pregnancies. Furthermore, patients are less likely to encounter complications that might arise with more invasive surgeries. Typically, women observe decreased blood loss and minimized physical trauma, permitting a swifter return to their daily activities. At Viva Eve, we highlight these benefits to help our patients make informed and empowered healthcare choices.

Care and Recovery Following Vaginal Myomectomy

After the procedure, patients are attentively monitored to facilitate a seamless recovery following vaginal myomectomy. It's crucial to adhere to the surgeon’s directives about activity limitations and follow-up visits. Most women can gradually return to light activities in a few days and engage in more intensive activities within several weeks, based on personal healing rates. At Viva Eve, our support continues well past the surgery, with various resources provided for both physical and emotional recovery.
