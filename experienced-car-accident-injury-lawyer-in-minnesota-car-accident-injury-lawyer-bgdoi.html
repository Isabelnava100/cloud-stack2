In Minnesota, the concept of comparative fault comes into play with personal injury claims, including those from car accidents. If you are found partially at fault for the accident, your compensation may be reduced by the percentage of your fault. For instance, if you are deemed 20% at fault and awarded $10,000, you would receive $8,000. Our attorneys will work diligently to mitigate any claims of shared fault during your case to maximize your compensation.

What types of damages can I recover in a personal injury lawsuit?
In a personal injury lawsuit, you can recover various types of damages, including economic and non-economic damages. Economic damages encompass medical bills, rehabilitation expenses, lost wages, and property damage costs. Non-economic damages include compensation for pain and suffering, emotional distress, and loss of enjoyment of life. Our knowledgeable attorneys at 612-Injured will ensure that all aspects of your losses are accounted for in your claim, striving to secure the maximum compensation possible.

What should I do immediately after a car accident?
Immediately after a car accident, prioritize your safety and the safety of others. Call emergency services if anyone is injured and ensure that you exchange information with the other parties involved. Take photos of the accident scene and gather witness contact information. Importantly, avoid admitting fault or discussing the details of the accident with anyone other than law enforcement or your legal representative.
