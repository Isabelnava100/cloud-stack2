What Is Paint Correction?

Paint correction is a meticulous process of removing imperfections from your car’s paintwork. These imperfections often include swirl marks, light scratches, oxidation, and other blemishes that affect the surface’s clarity and reflectivity. By using professional-grade polishing tools and compounds, skilled technicians can restore your car’s paint to its original brilliance.

Whether you’re dealing with minor scratches or significant defects, automotive paint correction is the ultimate solution for achieving that showroom shine.

The Paint Correction Process

Here’s a step-by-step overview of how paint correction is typically performed:

  1. Thorough Wash and Decontamination: The process begins with a deep clean to remove dirt, grime, and contaminants from the surface. This ensures that the polishing phase won’t grind particles into the paint.

  2. Inspection: A detailed inspection identifies the severity of the imperfections and determines the appropriate approach.

  3. Paint Leveling: Using specialized polishing machines, technicians carefully remove microscopic layers of clear coat to eliminate scratches and swirls.

  4. Polishing and Buffing: This stage restores the paint’s clarity and shine, leaving a flawless, mirror-like finish.

  5. Protection: To maintain the results, a layer of wax, sealant, or ceramic coating is applied to protect against future damage.
