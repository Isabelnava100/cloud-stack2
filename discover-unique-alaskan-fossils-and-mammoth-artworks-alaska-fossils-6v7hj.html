These fossils, including mammoth tusks, bones, and plant remains, represent a diverse range of species that once inhabited Alaska. They help scientists understand the climate, geography, and biological diversity of the area over millions of years. What type of maintenance do mammoth tusk art pieces require?
Mammoth tusk art pieces require minimal maintenance to preserve their beauty and integrity. We recommend placing the artworks in a stable environment, away from direct sunlight and extreme humidity, to prevent fading and damage. Periodic dusting with a soft cloth is advisable to keep the surface clean. In case of more pronounced cleaning needs, we suggest consulting with professionals who specialize in fossil restoration to avoid damaging the piece. Proper care will ensure that your Alaska fossils and mammoth tusk art last for generations.
