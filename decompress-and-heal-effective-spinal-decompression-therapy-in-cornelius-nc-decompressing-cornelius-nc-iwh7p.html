Understanding Spinal Decompression Therapy

Spinal decompression involves the gentle stretching of the spine to change its position and alleviate pressure on the spinal discs—the gel-like cushions between the bones in your spine. This shift creates negative pressure within the disc, promoting the retraction of bulging or herniated discs and facilitating the movement of water, oxygen, and nutrient-rich fluids into the discs, enhancing healing.

Conditions Treated by Spinal Decompression

Spinal decompression therapy is utilized to address various conditions, including:

  • Herniated or Bulging Discs: When discs become displaced or protrude, they can press on nerves, causing pain.

  • Degenerative Disc Disease: The wear and tear of discs over time can lead to reduced cushioning between vertebrae.

  • Sciatica: Compression of the sciatic nerve can result in pain radiating down the leg.

  • Posterior Facet Syndrome: Degeneration of facet joints can cause back pain.

  • Injured or Diseased Spinal Nerve Roots: Damage to nerve roots can lead to significant discomfort.

Benefits of Spinal Decompression Therapy

The advantages of spinal decompression therapy include:

  • Non-Surgical Approach: Offers a conservative treatment option before considering surgery.

  • Pain Relief: Reduces pressure on nerves, alleviating pain.

  • Improved Mobility: Enhances range of motion and overall function.

  • Promotes Healing: Facilitates the flow of nutrients to damaged discs, supporting recovery.

The Spinal Decompression Procedure

During a spinal decompression session at Lakeside Sports Chiropractic and Physical Therapy, patients are comfortably positioned on a specialized table. A harness is fitted around the pelvis and another around the trunk. The lower part of the table moves to provide traction and relaxation, creating a gentle stretching effect on the spine. Each session typically lasts between 30 to 45 minutes, and a complete treatment plan may involve multiple sessions over several weeks.

Is Spinal Decompression Right for You?

While spinal decompression therapy is effective for many, it may not be suitable for everyone. Individuals with fractures, tumors, abdominal aortic aneurysms, advanced osteoporosis, or certain medical conditions should consult with a healthcare professional before undergoing this therapy.
