Cracked Tooth Repair Explained

Repairing a cracked tooth is vital for stopping further deterioration and relieving discomfort. At Fleetwood Dental, we present a variety of restorative treatments customized for your situation. Options for Restoring Cracked Teeth

Fleetwood Dental specializes in various solutions tailored for cracked tooth repair. Dental crowns are ideal for substantial damage by yielding both durability and safeguarding features. For minor issues, bonding can enhance both the tooth's appearance and its effective usage. If the tooth’s nerve is at risk, root canal therapy may be necessary to preserve the tooth's vitality.

Frequently Asked Questions

Typical signs of a cracked tooth include sharp pain while eating, sensitivity to temperature changes, and swelling in your gum area.

To avoid having cracked teeth, maintain excellent oral hygiene practices and refrain from chewing on tough items. Consider wearing a mouthguard if you grind your teeth.

If you suspect a cracked tooth, consult Fleetwood Dental right away for a thorough examination and possible treatment alternatives.

Various treatment options for a cracked tooth may include dental crowns, bonding, root canal procedure, or extraction for severe cases.

The timeframe for a cracked tooth repair procedure is dependent on the selected treatment, varying from a singular appointment for bonding to multiple visits for crowns or root canal treatments.
