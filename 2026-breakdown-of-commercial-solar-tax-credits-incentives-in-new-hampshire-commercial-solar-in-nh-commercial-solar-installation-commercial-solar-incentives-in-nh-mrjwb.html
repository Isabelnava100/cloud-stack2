2026 Highlights of the Federal Investment Tax Credit (ITC)

The Federal Investment Tax Credit (ITC) remains an essential incentive for commercial solar undertakings. What is Offered in 2026? For qualifying press on commercial solar systems: Businesses can secure a federal tax credit of up to 30% for installations that begin before July 4, 2026. How to qualify for the full 30% ITC: - Initiate construction before July 4, 2026 - Ensure the system is fully operational and in service Be aware that federal solar incentives are susceptible to changes in policy, which may lead to future reductions. It benefits businesses to kick-off their projects sooner to preserve eligibility.

Understanding MACRS & 100% Bonus Depreciation for Commercial Solar

Apart from the ITC, organizations can gain from the Modified Accelerated Cost Recovery System (MACRS) for depreciation. New in 2026: Reinstatement of the 100% Bonus Depreciation for qualifying projects. This provision allows companies to: - Completely write off 100% of eligible solar assets within the first year - Implement a 5-year MACRS depreciation plan - Significantly minimize taxable profits - Dramatically enhance overall project ROI Essential Prequisite: - Projects should start operations between January 19, 2025, and January 1, 2030 to qualify for 100% bonus depreciation - Bonus depreciation is applied to the 5-year MACRS schedule Maximizing Benefits: Marrying ITC and Bonus Depreciation: Enterprises can pair the 30% ITC with 100% bonus depreciation refunds. That said, the original depreciation base must be reduced by half the ITC amount prior to depreciation application. Stacking these two benefits effectively can boost initial-year tax savings. Combined with the ITC, businesses can recoup 40% to 60% of their overall project costs in the initial year, contingent on tax situations and project configurations.
