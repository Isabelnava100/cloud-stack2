Frequently Asked Questions

A wrongful death claim gives the remaining relatives of a deceased person the opportunity to seek compensation for their losses caused by someone's negligent action or wrongful behavior.

Ordinarily, the spouse, children, or parents of the deceased have the right to file a wrongful death claim. In certain situations, other dependents or individuals may also be included.

Common reasons encompass automobile collisions, medical negligence, workplace incidents, flawed products, and deliberate acts of harm.

In Connecticut, the timeframe to submit a wrongful death claim generally extends to two years from the date the individual passed away.

Families may seek compensatory damages that include loss of earnings, funeral expenses, and non-economic losses like companionship.
