Common Questions

Legal entities refer to various organizational forms like LLCs, S-Corps, and C-Corps that businesses utilize for operations. The selection of these forms heavily impacts tax responsibilities, making it vital to choose wisely to minimize tax exposure.

By strategically selecting and overseeing your legal entities, you can enhance tax treatment, classify revenues effectively, and evade excessive self-employment taxes through tailored approaches that suit your operational needs.

Companies can select from several legal formats, including Limited Liability Companies (LLCs), S Corporations, C Corporations, partnerships, and trusts, all of which carry distinct tax implications.

Phantom income arises from transactions between entities reporting earnings without genuine cash movement. If these are not managed correctly through proper documentation, they can lead to significant tax liabilities.

Indeed, various legal entities are subjected to differing tax rates and requirements. For instance, C Corporations are liable for double taxation, whereas S Corporations permit pass-through taxation benefiting individual owners.
