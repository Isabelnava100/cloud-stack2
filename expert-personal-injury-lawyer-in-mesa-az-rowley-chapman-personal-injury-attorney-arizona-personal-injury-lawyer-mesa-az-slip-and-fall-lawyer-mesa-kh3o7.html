Common Questions

After an accident, seek medical attention, gather evidence, document the incident, and reach out to a personal injury lawyer as soon as possible.

A personal injury lawyer will assess your case, advise you on legal rights, gather evidence, negotiate with insurance companies, and represent you in court if necessary.

In Arizona, you generally have two years from the date of the accident to file a personal injury claim.

You may be eligible for compensation for medical expenses, lost wages, pain and suffering, emotional distress, and property damage.

Most personal injury lawyers work on a contingency fee basis, meaning you only pay attorney fees if you win your case.
