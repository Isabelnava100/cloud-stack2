Understanding Purity and Third-Party Testing

In the realm of research peptides, purity is a critical factor that cannot be overlooked. Researchers should always request a certificate of analysis (COA) from suppliers to confirm the purity levels of their peptides. This documentation should provide details on the method of analysis, typically HPLC, outlining the peptide's purity percentage to verify that it meets established laboratory standards. Transparency in testing ensures that researchers are equipped with the quality necessary to conduct reliable experiments.
