Understanding Fully Insured Health Plans

Fully insured health plans provide a straightforward option for companies looking to obtain health insurance from a carrier. Under this model, employers pay a fixed premium, and the insurance company assumes the risk of covering medical claims. This option is beneficial for businesses wanting predictable costs and minimal administrative burdens. However, it may not always provide the most cost-effective solutions for companies with 50 to 500 employees requiring extensive healthcare needs.

Exploring Level-Funded Health Insurance

Level-funded health insurance combines the features of self-funding and traditional fully insured plans. Companies pay a fixed monthly fee that includes the cost of claims and administrative expenses, which can result in savings for businesses with low healthcare utilization. This model provides greater flexibility in managing costs while giving access to comprehensive coverage. CFH Insurance Consultants can help assess if this option suits your company's size and health profile.

Navigating Self-Funded Health Plans

Self-funded health plans give employers more control over their healthcare spending by allowing them to directly pay for claims instead of purchasing traditional insurance. This approach can provide significant savings for mid-sized businesses with predictable healthcare costs, and it allows for customizable benefit plans that meet specific employee needs. By working with an independent broker like CFH Insurance Consultants, companies can uncover the best self-funding strategies tailored to their workforce in Michigan.
