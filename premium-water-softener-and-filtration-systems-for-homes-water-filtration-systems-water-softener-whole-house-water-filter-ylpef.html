Frequently Asked Questions

A water softener is a system designed to remove hardness minerals like calcium and magnesium from your water. It works by replacing these minerals with sodium, preventing scale buildup in pipes and improving water quality.

The replacement frequency for your whole house water filter depends on your water usage and quality. Typically, filters should be changed every 6 to 12 months for optimal performance.

SoftPro water softeners utilize upflow regeneration technology and precision salt-saving valves, making them more efficient than traditional systems. They are designed with the latest NSF-certified components for durability and effective performance.

While some homeowners may choose to install a water softener themselves, we recommend seeking professional installation for optimal performance and to avoid potential issues.

Using a water filtration system helps eliminate contaminants such as chlorine, lead, and sediment from your water, providing cleaner, safer drinking water which can promote better overall health.

How do I know if I need a water softener or a filtration system?
If you are experiencing hard water problems, such as scale buildup or dry skin, a water softener is recommended. If you're more concerned about contamination, a whole house water filter is the best choice to ensure safe drinking and cooking water.
