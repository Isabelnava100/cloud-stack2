How do you measure student success at Country Day Montessori?
At Country Day Montessori, we measure student success through various assessment methods, including standardized tests like the IOWA test. The majority of our students perform at or above grade level, showcasing the effectiveness of our Montessori approach. We also closely track each child's progress through observational assessments and individualized learning plans. Our goal is to cultivate a genuine love for learning, enabling students to not only meet academic benchmarks but also thrive in their personal growth and development.

Can parents participate in their child's learning experience at CDM?
Absolutely! At Country Day Montessori, we believe that parental involvement is crucial for a child's educational journey. We encourage parents to engage in various ways, including volunteering in the classroom, participating in school events, and attending parent-teacher conferences. We also provide resources and activities for families to do at home that align with the Montessori philosophy. This collaboration ensures a comprehensive learning experience for children and strengthens the bond between home and school.
