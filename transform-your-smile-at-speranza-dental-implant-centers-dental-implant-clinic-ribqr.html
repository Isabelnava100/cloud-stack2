Frequently Asked Questions

Dental implants are titanium posts that are surgically placed into the jawbone to serve as a stable foundation for replacement teeth. They fuse with the bone over time, providing a strong and lasting support system.

The duration of the dental implant procedure can vary, but typically it can take several months from the initial consultation to the final restoration. This includes healing times for the jawbone and gums.

Most patients report feeling little to no pain during the dental implant procedure due to anesthesia. Post-procedure discomfort is usually manageable with over-the-counter pain relief.

With proper care and regular dental check-ups, dental implants can last for many years, often decades. Good oral hygiene is essential for their longevity.

Candidates for dental implants should have enough bone density in their jaw to support the implants. A consultation at Speranza Dental Implant Centers will help determine your eligibility.

Extra FAQ

What are the benefits of dental implants over dentures?

Dental implants offer a more permanent solution compared to dentures, providing better stability, comfort, and functionality, enabling you to eat and speak naturally.

How do I care for my dental implants?

Caring for dental implants involves maintaining good oral hygiene, including regular brushing and flossing, and scheduling routine dental check-ups with our team.
