Benefits of Cumaru Decking

Cumaru decking is renowned for its striking appearance, featuring a rich golden-brown hue that adds warmth to any patio or deck. Its impressive Janka hardness rating of 3540 makes it one of the hardest woods available, ensuring it can withstand heavy foot traffic and resist wear and tear. The natural oil content in Cumaru not only enhances its beauty but also provides inherent resistance to moisture, making it ideal for outdoor use. Additionally, its slip-resistant surface ensures safety during rainy days, making it a prudent choice for homeowners seeking both style and functionality.

Comparing Cumaru and IPE: Which is Better?

When comparing Cumaru lumber to IPE, both are highly rated as premium hardwoods, yet Cumaru tends to be a more cost-effective option without sacrificing quality. While IPE is known for its deep brown shades, Cumaru offers a unique golden hue that many find equally appealing. Both woods have resistance to decay and insects, but Cumaru's higher availability can translate to lower pricing and easier access for customers. For those seeking a luxurious deck, Cumaru decking provides a stunning visual impact with long-lasting performance, making it an excellent competitive alternative to IPE.

Caring for Your Cumaru Deck

Maintaining Cumaru decking is simple and straightforward, requiring minimal upkeep to preserve its beauty and durability. Regular sweeping and occasional washing with mild soap and water can keep your deck looking pristine. If you desire a more uniform color, applying a high-quality UV-blocking finish can enhance its natural luster and protect against sun damage. With proper care, Cumaru decking can last for decades, providing functional beauty to your outdoor spaces for years to come.
